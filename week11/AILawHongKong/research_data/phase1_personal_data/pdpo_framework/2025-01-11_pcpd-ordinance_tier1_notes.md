## Document Summary

### Overview
**The Personal Data (Privacy) Ordinance (PDPO)** was passed in 1995 and took effect from December 1996. It is one of Asia's longest standing comprehensive data protection laws, based on:
- **August 1994 Law Reform Commission Report:** "Reform of the Law Relating to the Protection of Personal Data"
- **OECD Privacy Guidelines 1980**
- **Directive 95/46/EC** (EU, referenced in draft version)

**Major Amendments:**
- **2012:** Introduction of direct marketing provisions and additional protection measures
- **2021:** Anti-doxxing amendments, criminalisation of doxxing acts, enhanced enforcement powers for Privacy Commissioner

---

### Key Definitions

1. **Personal Data**
   - Information which relates to a **living individual**
   - Can be used to **identify** that individual
   - Must exist in a form which **access to or processing** is practicable

2. **Data Subject**
   - The individual who is the subject of the personal data

3. **Data User**
   - Person who, either alone or jointly, **controls** the collection, holding, processing, or use of personal data

4. **Data Processor**
   - Person who processes personal data **on behalf of another person** (a data user)
   - **Not directly regulated** under PDPO
   - Data users must ensure processors meet PDPO requirements through contractual means

---

## Six Data Protection Principles (DPPs)

### DPP1: Purpose and Manner of Collection
**Requirements:**
- Collect only for a **lawful purpose** directly related to data user's function/activity
- Data collected should be **necessary and adequate but not excessive**
- Collection means must be **lawful and fair**

**Data Subject Notification (for direct collection):**
- Whether supplying data is **obligatory or voluntary**
- **Purpose** of using the data
- **Classes of persons** to whom data may be transferred
- **Right and means** to request access and correction

---

### DPP2: Accuracy and Duration of Retention
**Requirements:**
- Take all practicable steps to ensure data is **accurate**
- Data must **not be kept longer than necessary** for fulfillment of purpose

**Section 26 Requirement:**
- Data users must **erase personal data** that is no longer required for the purpose
- **Exception:** Unless erasure is prohibited by law or is not in the public interest
- **Penalty for contravention:** Fine up to **HK$10,000**

**Data Processor Obligations:**
- Data users must ensure processors comply with retention requirements through contractual means

---

### DPP3: Use of Data
**Prohibition:**
- Cannot use personal data for **new purpose unrelated to original purpose**
- **Exception:** With data subject's **express and voluntary consent**
- Data subjects can **withdraw consent** by written notice

**Part 6A: Direct Marketing Requirements**

**Informed Consent Requirements:**
Must inform data subject of:
- Intention to use data for direct marketing
- That data user **cannot use data without consent**
- **Kinds of personal data** to be used
- **Classes of marketing subjects** involved
- **Right to opt out**

**If Transferring to Third Party:**
Additional requirements:
- Inform of **intention to transfer**
- **Classes of transferees**
- **Classes of marketing subjects**
- **Whether transfer is for gain**

**Penalties:**
- Standard: Fine of **HK$500,000** and imprisonment for **3 years**
- If data provided to third party for gain: Fine of **HK$1,000,000** and imprisonment for **5 years**

---

### DPP4: Data Security
**Requirements:**
- Take **all practicable steps** to protect personal data against:
  - Unauthorised or accidental **access**
  - Unauthorised or accidental **processing**
  - Unauthorised or accidental **erasure**
  - Unauthorised or accidental **loss or use**

**Considerations:**
- Nature of the data
- Potential harm if security breach occurs
- Measures for ensuring **integrity, prudence, and competence** of persons with data access

**Data Processor Obligations:**
- Must adopt contractual means to ensure processors comply with security requirements

---

### DPP5: Openness and Transparency
**Requirements:**
- Take all practicable steps to ensure **openness** of:
  - Personal data **policies and practices**
  - **Kind of personal data** held
  - **Main purposes** for holding data

---

### DPP6: Access and Correction
**Rights of Data Subjects:**
- **Request access** to their own personal data
- **Request correction** of their own personal data

**Data User Obligations:**
- Must give **reasons when refusing** access or correction requests

**Part 5 Detailed Provisions:**
- **Manner and timeframe** for compliance with data access requests
- **Manner and timeframe** for compliance with data correction requests
- **Circumstances** in which data user may refuse requests
- **Log book requirement:** Must record all refusals made

## Exemptions

**Purpose of Exemptions:**
Balance data privacy rights against other important rights or public interest

**Common Exemption Scenarios:**
- Crime prevention or prosecution
- Security and defence
- Statistics and research
- News activity
- Protecting a data subject's health
- Required or authorised by **law or court order**
- Required for **exercising or defending legal rights** in Hong Kong

**Important Notes:**
- Exemptions are **defences** for data users to avoid liability
- Should **not be routinely relied upon** - consider case-by-case
- **Data user must prove** exemption applies
- Exemption does not **create obligation** to rely on it
- No one can **compel** data user to rely on exemption

## Enforcement Mechanisms

### Privacy Commissioner Powers

**Investigation Powers:**
- Receive complaints about PDPO breaches
- Conduct investigations when reasonable grounds exist
- Publish investigation reports if in public interest

**Enforcement Actions:**
- Issue **enforcement notices** directing remedial/preventive steps
- Carry out **criminal investigation** for certain offences
- **Institute prosecution** in Commissioner's own name for:
  - Section 64 offences (doxxing-related)
  - Certain other relevant offences
- Refer cases to **Police or Department of Justice** for follow-up

**Penalties for Non-Compliance with Enforcement Notice:**
- First conviction: Maximum fine **HK$50,000** + imprisonment **2 years** + daily penalty **HK$1,000**
- Subsequent convictions: Maximum fine **HK$100,000** + imprisonment **2 years** + daily penalty **HK$2,000**

---

### Criminal Offences Under PDPO

**Key Offences:**
- **Section 26:** Failure to erase data no longer required (Fine up to HK$10,000)
- **Section 64:** Doxxing-related offences
- **Direct marketing provisions:** Non-compliance (HK$500,000 - HK$1,000,000 + imprisonment)

**Important Note:**
- Contravention of a **DPP is NOT an offence** (but may trigger enforcement notice)
- Contravention of **specific statutory provisions IS an offence**

**Reference Document:**

### Civil Remedies

**Data Subject Rights:**
- May seek **compensation by civil action** for damage caused by PDPO contravention
- **Privacy Commissioner may provide legal assistance** if deemed appropriate

---

### Proactive Powers

**Inspection Powers:**
- Proactively inspect personal data systems
- Make recommendations for compliance enhancement

**Code of Practice Powers:**
- Issue codes of practice providing practical guidance
- Non-compliance with code is **not an offence**
- But can be **proof of contravention** of relevant PDPO requirement

## Key Compliance Takeaways

### For Data Users (Organizations)
1. **Understand all 6 DPPs** - They form the foundation of PDPO compliance
2. **Implement data collection notices** - Inform individuals when collecting data directly
3. **Establish data retention policies** - Don't keep data longer than necessary
4. **Get informed consent for direct marketing** - Before using or transferring data
5. **Implement data security measures** - Protect against unauthorized access
6. **Be transparent** - Publish privacy policies and practices
7. **Respond to access/correction requests** - Maintain logbook of refusals
8. **Use contracts with data processors** - Ensure they comply with PDPO requirements
9. **Don't rely on exemptions routinely** - Consider case-by-case

### For Individuals (Data Subjects)
1. **Right to be informed** - About data collection purpose and use
2. **Right of access** - Can request own personal data
3. **Right of correction** - Can request correction of inaccuracies
4. **Right to opt-out** - From direct marketing
5. **Right to withdraw consent** - For data use (by written notice)
6. **Right to complain** - To Privacy Commissioner if rights violated
7. **Right to compensation** - Through civil action for damages
